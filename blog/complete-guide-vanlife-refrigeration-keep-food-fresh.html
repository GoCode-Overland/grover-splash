Frequently Asked Questions From Our Community

How much power does a van fridge actually use?

We've been tracking this with our community for years. Most 12V compressor fridges draw 3-6 amps when actively cooling, cycling on and off based on temperature. Daily consumption typically ranges from 30-60 amp-hours, depending on outside temperature and how often you open the door.

Real community data: Our users report an average of 45 Ah/day for a 60L unit under normal vanlife conditions. Many use Grover's üìç pin creation to mark spots where their power consumption was particularly efficient.

Why isn't my van fridge working?

This is probably our most common support question. Here's our community's troubleshooting checklist that solves 90% of issues:

Check these first:

  • Battery voltage ‚Äì Needs 11.5V+ to start, 12V+ to run efficiently
  • Fuse integrity ‚Äì Both main and inline fuses
  • Wire connections ‚Äì Corrosion at terminals kills more fridges than anything
  • Ventilation clearance ‚Äì Blocked vents cause overheating and shutdown

Can I run a van fridge off solar alone?

Absolutely! Most of our community runs their vanlife refrigeration purely on solar. The key is proper system sizing.

What works for our users:

  • 400W solar minimum for reliable operation
  • 600W+ for hot climates or heavy usage
  • Lithium batteries for better efficiency and depth of discharge

Success story: "Been running my Dometic CRX110E on 600W of solar and 400Ah lithium for two years. Never had a power issue, even during week-long cloudy stretches in the Pacific Northwest. Can I install a van fridge myself?

Absolutely! Most of our community members install their own fridges, and honestly, it's not as complicated as you might think. The key is taking your time with the electrical connections and ventilation setup.

What you'll need:

  • Basic electrical tools and multimeter
  • Hole saw for ventilation (if needed)
  • Proper wire sizing for your fridge's amp draw
  • Marine-grade electrical connections

When to call a pro: If you're not comfortable with electrical work or need to modify your van's structure significantly, it's worth hiring a professional. Many of our community members found local RV shops that specialize in van conversions through our network.
